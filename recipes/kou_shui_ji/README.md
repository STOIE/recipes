# Kou Shui Ji (Sichuan Cold Chicken)

### Meat Prep

#### Ingredients

* 1kg chicken, either whole thighs or whole breasts (skin on is better if possible), can be bone-in or boned-out.
* 1-1/2 tablespoons grated ginger
* 1 teaspoon salt
* 1-1/2 tablespoons shaoxing wine

#### Method

1. Add the ginger, salt and wine to the chicken, then mix well and set aside, can be left overnight to marinade if desired.
1. Then place the chicken into a steamer and steam covered for around 90 minutes.
1. Once cooked, remove from the steamer and set aside the juices in another bowl.
1. Next, slice the chicken into long thin slices (across the grain), can be chopped with a cleaver if bone-in.
1. Allow the chicken to cool.

---

### Sauce

#### Ingredients

* 120ml (1/2 cup) of chicken drippings
* 3 tablespoons chinese black vinegar
* 3 tablespoons of light soy sauce (zhongba preferred)
* 2 tablespoons of brown sugar
* 1-1/2 tablespoons sichuan pepper oil
* 1 tablespoon chinese sesame paste
* 2 teaspoons sesame oil
* 80ml (1/3 cup) chilli oil (not chilli crisp)

#### Method

1. Place all the ingredients into a small bowl and give a good stir, then set aside to use later.

---

### The Dish

#### Ingredients

* 30 grams peanuts (1/4 cup) peanuts, raw
* 2 scallions, green only, thinly sliced
* 1 cucumber, sliced into 5mm thick diagonal slices
* 1 teaspoon toasted white sesame seeds

#### Method

1. First roast your peanuts, either by tradionally frying them in oil in a wok, or in the oven on a tray.
1. Once roasted, remove all the skins from the peanuts and set aside.
1. Take as many bowls as desired, generally 3-4 per kg chicken.
1. Place the sliced cucumbers into the bottom of your bowls.
1. Place the sliced chicken on top of the cucumber slices in each bowl.
1. Pour the sauce over the chicken into each bowl.
1. Then place the peanuts, scallions and sesame seeds atop the chicken in each bowl.
1. Now serve cold as is.
