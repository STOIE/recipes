# Bacon

## Regular

#### Ingredients

(per kg of pork belly, but scale to your needs)

* 1kg rindless pork belly
* 20 grams brown sugar
* 25 grams salt
* 2.5 grams no.1 curing salt
* 0.5 grams ascorbic acid


#### Method

1. Mix the brown sugar, salt and curing salt together thoroughly in a bowl (do not mix in the ascorbic acid).
1. Layout the pork belly on a bench and pat dry is wet.
1. Immediately before using, thoroughly mix the ascorbic acid into the sugar mixture, then thoroughly coat all sides of the pork belly, ensuring you use all the mixture fully.
1. Either vacuum bag each belly, or place into a tub with a rack standing off the bottom (bellies can be stacked on top of one another on the rack).
1. Place into the fridge between 0-4C for ~7 days (~7 days for a regular thickness belly, up to 10 days for thicker bellies).
1. Remove from fridge (and any packaging) then smoke (high smoke - high airflow) for 4 hours at 150 deg F MAX (65 deg C), we need to ensure temp remains low (cold smoke) so fat doesn't render.
1. Then increase temp to 175 deg F MAX (79 deg C) and cook to internal temp of 135 deg F (57 deg C) (taken at thickest part of thickest belly, basically coldest part), once again, still a "cold" smoke.
1. Once done, remove and place onto a cooling rack for 30 minutes to cool down, before refridgerating uncovered overnight.
1. Once refridgerated, remove and slice to desired thickness.
1. Bacon can then be either cooked, or stored (vac sealed and refridgerated or frozen).
